History of Linus Torvalds

Early Years

Born in Helsinki, Finland in 1969, Linus Torvalds showed an early interest in computers. His journey began with the Commodore VIC-20, a home computer that sparked his passion for programming. This early exposure to computing would later influence his approach to software development.

Linux Creation

In 1991, as a computer science student at the University of Helsinki, Linus began working on his own operating system kernel. What started as a personal project quickly grew into something much larger. The first version of Linux was released on September 17, 1991, marking the beginning of a revolution in open-source software.

Project Growth

Linux quickly became an international project, gathering thousands of programmers from around the world. Under Linus's leadership, the Linux kernel has grown into one of the largest collaborative software projects in history. His unique management style, known as "benevolent dictator for life," has helped maintain the project's focus and quality.

Legacy

Linus Torvalds's contributions to computing have earned him numerous awards and recognition. He continues to lead the Linux kernel development while also creating Git, the most widely used version control system. His work has fundamentally changed how software is developed and distributed.
